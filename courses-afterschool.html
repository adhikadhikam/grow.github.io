Course Curriculum

  • Phonics

    English letters have a distinction between the name of the letter and the sound of the sentence. It is phonics that guides the sound rules of English spelling in order. For example, in the case of “Inu” in Japanese, “I” reads “I”, “Nu” reads “Nu”, and can be understood as a dog. However, even if you read “dog” written in English as “D-Orgy”, the meaning is not clear. That's because English reads with the sound of letters, not the names of letters. It is said that if you know the rules of reading d as [d], o as [aa], and g as [g], you can theoretically read the words you see for the first time. In a word, phonics is "English spelling and sound rules".

  • TPR

    TPR is an abbreviation for Total Physical Response. It is a training method that aims to understand and act directly in English what you have heard in English. While the baby learns words, the mother does not teach and teach grammar, but she learns words by speaking naturally and listening to conversations. TPR is based on this reasoning and has the effect of creating a neural circuit dedicated to English without having to translate Japanese into English in the head. We don't always speak grammatically when we speak Japanese. We can do that because we have a Japanese neural network. In order to be able to speak English even if you understand it, you need a neural circuit dedicated to English. This neural circuit can understand English as it is, so it is very useful not only for conversations but also for long sentences.

  • Dolce Words / Sight Words

    Dolce words (Sight words) are “important words that can only be seen and remembered” that do not fit the rules of Phonics. Dolce words have nouns, verbs, and adjectives regardless of parts of speech, but the common point is that they are function words that are indispensable for reading sentences. Eg) as, could, when, them etc ... It is very important to keep Dolce words together with Phonics. At Glow International Preschool, children will learn Phonics and Dolce words while enjoying nature.
